About Me

My journey into the world of bioinformatics began in 2015 when I first encountered the fascinating intersection of biology and computer science. What started as a curious exploration during my undergraduate studies has evolved into a full-fledged passion for unraveling the mysteries of life through data. Over the years, I've had the privilege of contributing to groundbreaking research projects at esteemed institutions like Harvard Medical School and Brigham and Women's Hospital, where I honed my skills in advanced genomic analysis and machine learning integration.

Today, as a seasoned professional based in Boston with a Master's degree in Bioinformatics, a graduate certificate in Data Analytics, and a perfect 4.0 GPA, the passion for leveraging data to drive scientific breakthroughs remains unwavering. Expertise extends to designing innovative algorithms for biological data interpretation and deploying scalable solutions on cloud infrastructures like AWS. From advancing single-cell analysis at Fulcrum Therapeutics or exploring Machine Learning applications at The Institute for Experiential AI, each experience has reinforced a commitment to pushing the boundaries of healthcare and biotechnology.

My main focus these days is on decoding the intricacies of alternative splicing and its role in disease mechanisms. I thrive in the sweet spot where biology meets data science – where complex biological problems are transformed into elegant computational solutions. Whether it's diving deep into genomic data, crafting efficient algorithms, or bridging the gap between wet-lab and computational biology, I'm always excited to take on new challenges in the ever-evolving field of bioinformatics.
